# Where Overdose Is Heading


![Where Overdose Is Heading](/courses/overdose-new-starter/assets/lesson-images/m6_02_future.jpg)

Understanding where the company is going helps you see where your career might go. Overdose has grown dramatically since 2016, but we're far from done. Let's look at the trajectory and what it means for you.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Understand Overdose's growth trajectory and ambitions
- Identify major trends shaping our future
- Recognize opportunities created by company growth
- Align your career with company direction

## The Story So Far

Let's recap the journey to appreciate the momentum:

**2016:** Two founders, a basement, $500k revenue
**2018:** 50+ people, expanded to Australia
**2020:** 220+ people, 8 global offices
**2024:** 450+ people, industry-leading capability

That's not incremental growth — that's transformation. And the trajectory continues.

## Where We're Going

While specific plans evolve, some directions are clear:

### Geographic Expansion
We're growing our presence in key markets:

**Americas:** Significant growth opportunity. The US is the world's largest ecommerce market, and we're building deeper capability to serve it.

**Europe:** Selective expansion into European markets where demand for Complete Commerce exists.

**APAC:** Continued strengthening of our home market leadership.

### Capability Building
We're deepening expertise in strategic areas:

**AI & Personalization:** Machine learning is transforming commerce. We're building capability to help clients leverage AI effectively.

**Composable Commerce:** Headless and API-first architecture is growing. Our Commercetools and composable expertise positions us for this shift.

**B2B Commerce:** Digital transformation in B2B is massive. We're expanding our B2B capabilities to capture this opportunity.

**Data & Analytics:** As commerce becomes more data-driven, our analytics capabilities become more valuable.


### Market Position
We're cementing our position as the leading Anti-Agency:

**Enterprise Up-Market:** Moving toward larger, more complex clients who value Complete Commerce integration.

**Thought Leadership:** Establishing ourselves as industry leaders through content, speaking, and innovation.

**Platform Partnerships:** Deepening relationships with key platforms to maintain top-tier partner status.

## Industry Trends Shaping Our Future

External forces are creating opportunities:

### Commerce Is Everywhere
Ecommerce isn't just websites anymore. It's social commerce, live shopping, voice commerce, in-store digital. Omnichannel is the baseline, and it's getting more complex.

**What this means for OD:** More complexity = more need for integrated expertise. Our Complete Commerce model becomes more valuable.

### Technology Is Fragmenting
The best-of-breed approach is winning. Instead of monolithic platforms, brands are assembling commerce stacks from specialized tools.

**What this means for OD:** Integration expertise becomes critical. Our platform-agnostic, composable approach positions us well.

### Data Privacy Is Changing
Cookie deprecation, privacy regulations, first-party data focus. The rules of digital marketing are being rewritten.

**What this means for OD:** Clients need help navigating these changes. Our data and marketing expertise is essential.

### AI Is Transforming Everything
From personalization to content creation to customer service, AI is changing what's possible in commerce.

**What this means for OD:** We need to help clients leverage AI effectively while maintaining human judgment and creativity.

### Talent Is The Bottleneck
The biggest constraint for many brands isn't budget — it's capability. In-house teams can't keep up with the pace of change.

**What this means for OD:** Demand for our services grows as the talent gap widens.

## What Growth Means For You

Company growth creates individual opportunity:

### More Roles Opening
Growth means new positions at all levels. Leadership roles, specialist roles, new function roles. If you're performing, opportunities appear.

### Bigger Scope
As we take on larger clients and more complex work, scope expands. The ceiling on what you can accomplish rises.

### Geographic Options
More offices means more options for where to work. Career opportunities aren't limited to your current location.

### Learning From Scale
Bigger, more complex projects mean more interesting problems. You'll learn things impossible at smaller scale.

### Financial Upside
Company growth typically means better compensation opportunities, profit sharing potential, and career advancement that brings financial rewards.

## Risks and Realities

Growth isn't without challenges:

### Growing Pains
Rapid growth strains systems, processes, and culture. What worked at 50 people doesn't work at 450. Adaptation is constant.

### Competition
We're not the only ones seeing these opportunities. Competition for clients and talent is intense.

### Market Volatility
Economic conditions affect client spending. Growth isn't linear — there are ups and downs.

### Culture Preservation
Maintaining what makes OD special while growing requires intentional effort. Culture can dilute if not protected.

> **Important:** Growth is opportunity AND challenge. Success requires navigating both.
