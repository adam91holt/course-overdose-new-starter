## Tupperware Australia: Digital Transformation

### The Challenge
Tupperware Australia had a legacy approach to sales — primarily party-based and direct selling. They needed to build a modern ecommerce presence that complemented (not competed with) their traditional model.

### What We Did
We built a new ecommerce website that integrated with their existing business model. The site needed to handle party ordering, individual purchases, and consultant referrals — all while maintaining the brand experience.

### The Results
**In under 6 months, sales made online via the new eCommerce website increased by nearly 100% from Q1 to Q2.**

That's not incremental improvement — that's transformation. By creating a digital channel that worked with their business model, we unlocked revenue that simply wasn't accessible before.

### The Lesson
Digital transformation doesn't mean abandoning what works. It means extending it. Understanding the client's existing business model was essential to designing a solution that enhanced rather than disrupted.

## ECOYA: Elevating Brand Experience

### The Challenge
ECOYA, a premium home fragrance brand, needed their Shopify stores in Australia and New Zealand to reflect their brand positioning. The existing experience wasn't doing justice to the product or creating the emotional connection that drives premium purchases.

### What We Did
Our New Zealand team transformed their Shopify stores into "captivating online destinations." The focus was on enhancing customer experience to elevate brand loyalty and drive repeat purchases.

**Key elements:**
- Visual storytelling that matched the sensory nature of the products
- Improved navigation and product discovery
- Enhanced mobile experience
- Customer journey optimizations

### The Results
Improved customer experience metrics, increased engagement, and stronger repeat purchase rates. When customers feel the brand online, they buy more and come back more often.

### The Lesson
For premium brands, experience isn't decoration — it's commercial imperative. Every touchpoint either reinforces or undermines the brand position. We treated the website as a brand experience, not just a transaction point.

## SABA: Composable Commerce Innovation

### The Challenge
SABA, a premium fashion brand, was ready for the next generation of commerce technology. They needed an architecture that would give them flexibility, speed, and best-in-class capabilities across all touchpoints.

### What We Did
We helped SABA embrace composable commerce — building their stack with:
- **Commercetools** for headless commerce backend
- **Algolia** for search and discovery
- **Klaviyo** for email marketing
- **Omneo** for customer loyalty
- **Comestri** for product information management

This best-of-breed approach meant SABA could use the right tool for each job, integrated into a cohesive whole.

### The Results
A future-proof architecture that gives SABA flexibility to evolve without ripping and replacing their entire stack. When better tools emerge in any category, they can swap components without rebuilding everything.

### The Lesson
Composable commerce isn't for everyone, but for brands with the technical capability and ambition, it offers unmatched flexibility. The key is tight integration — best-of-breed only works when the components actually talk to each other.

## Overland Footwear Group: Omnichannel Excellence

### The Challenge
Overland Footwear Group operates multiple footwear brands across retail stores and online channels. They needed a platform that could handle multi-brand complexity while delivering unified customer experiences.

### What We Did
Using Adobe Commerce and Klaviyo, we built a system that:
- Supports multiple brands from one platform
- Integrates online and in-store experiences
- Manages complex inventory across channels
- Delivers personalized marketing at scale

### The Results
Operational efficiency across brands, improved customer experience regardless of touchpoint, and the ability to scale without multiplying complexity.

### The Lesson
Omnichannel isn't just about having both stores and a website. It's about treating them as one business, one customer relationship, one inventory pool. The technology has to support that unified view.

## 3 Wise Men: Digital Reach Expansion

### The Challenge
3 Wise Men, a men's fashion brand, needed to expand their digital presence and improve their ability to acquire and convert customers online.

### What We Did
We implemented:
- **Adobe Commerce** as the platform foundation
- **Nosto** for AI-powered personalization
- **Algolia** for intelligent search

This combination meant customers could find what they wanted faster and see recommendations that actually matched their preferences.

### The Results
Expanded digital reach, improved conversion rates, and better customer engagement through personalized experiences.

### The Lesson
Personalization isn't a nice-to-have anymore — it's expected. Customers who see relevant products buy more. The technology exists to deliver this at scale; the question is whether you're using it.

## James Lane & Sleeping Giant: UGC Power

### The Challenge
Bart Group brands James Lane and Sleeping Giant (homewares and furniture) needed to boost customer engagement and leverage social proof to drive conversions.

### What We Did
We implemented Yotpo's reviews and user-generated content platform, then optimized how that content was displayed and used across the customer journey.

### The Results
Recognition in the Yotpo Partner Awards 2024 for driving customer engagement and revenue growth through effective use of reviews and UGC.

### The Lesson
Other customers are your best salespeople. Reviews, photos, and testimonials from real buyers build trust that no marketing copy can match. The key is collecting this content systematically and displaying it effectively.

## Common Patterns Across Case Studies

Looking at these stories, some patterns emerge:

**Integration wins.** Whether it's integrating channels, tools, or disciplines, connected thinking beats siloed execution.

**Results take time.** These transformations didn't happen overnight. They required sustained effort and continuous optimization.

**Technology enables, but doesn't solve.** Every case study involves technology, but the technology is in service of strategy and execution. Platforms don't create success — people using platforms well create success.

**Client partnership matters.** Every successful case study involves clients who engaged as partners, not just buyers.
