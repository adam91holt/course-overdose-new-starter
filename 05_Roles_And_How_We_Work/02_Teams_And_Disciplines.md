# Teams & Disciplines

Overdose is organized around disciplines — specialized areas of expertise that come together to deliver Complete Commerce. Understanding each discipline helps you collaborate effectively, regardless of where you sit.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Identify the main disciplines at Overdose
- Understand what each team does and how they contribute
- Know how to engage with different disciplines
- Recognize the career paths within each area

## The Strategy Team

Strategy is where client engagements begin and where they're continuously guided.

### What Strategy Does
- Leads discovery and understanding of client businesses
- Develops commerce strategies and roadmaps
- Manages ongoing client relationships
- Ensures all work aligns with business goals
- Advises clients on priorities and investments

### Roles In Strategy
**Commerce Strategists** — Develop strategic recommendations and roadmaps. Deep thinkers who translate business problems into actionable plans.

**Client Partners** — Own client relationships. Ensure partnerships are healthy, work is impactful, and clients are satisfied.

**Business Analysts** — Dive into data and research to support strategic recommendations. Turn information into insights.

### Working With Strategy
- They set the context for your work — understand their briefs
- They need your expertise input — share what you know
- They translate client feedback — listen to their guidance
- They're accountable for overall outcomes — help them succeed

## The Experience Team

Experience covers all aspects of design, user experience, and conversion optimization.

### What Experience Does
- Designs user interfaces and experiences
- Maps and optimizes customer journeys
- Runs conversion rate optimization programs
- Conducts user research and testing
- Maintains design systems and standards

### Roles In Experience
**UX Designers** — Focus on how things work. User flows, wireframes, information architecture, usability.

**UI Designers** — Focus on how things look. Visual design, branding implementation, aesthetic execution.

**CRO Specialists** — Focus on making things convert. A/B testing, optimization experiments, data-driven improvements.

**UX Researchers** — Focus on understanding users. Research studies, user testing, insights synthesis.

### Working With Experience
- They need requirements and constraints — be clear about technical limitations
- They need feedback on feasibility — tell them what's hard to build
- They produce specs you'll work from — ask questions if unclear
- They care deeply about user experience — advocate for users alongside them

## The Technology Team

Technology builds and maintains the platforms and systems that power commerce.

### What Technology Does
- Implements ecommerce platforms (Shopify, Adobe Commerce, BigCommerce, Commercetools)
- Builds custom functionality and integrations
- Ensures performance, security, and reliability
- Maintains and improves existing implementations
- Advises on technical architecture and decisions

### Roles In Technology
**Frontend Developers** — Build what users see and interact with. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, framework expertise.

**Backend Developers** — Build what powers things behind the scenes. Server-side logic, APIs, data handling.

**Solution Architects** — Design technical approaches and make architectural decisions. Bridge business requirements and technical implementation.

**Technical Leads** — Lead development teams. Code review, mentorship, technical direction.

**QA Engineers** — Ensure quality. Testing, bug identification, quality standards.

### Working With Technology
- They need clear requirements — ambiguity creates problems
- They need time estimates to be realistic — don't compress their timelines unfairly
- They need context on priorities — help them understand what matters most
- They have deep expertise — respect their technical judgment

## The Marketing Team

Marketing drives traffic, acquires customers, and builds ongoing relationships.

### What Marketing Does
- Runs paid media campaigns (search, social, display)
- Manages email and SMS marketing programs
- Implements marketing automation
- Develops customer segmentation and targeting
- Optimizes marketing efficiency and ROI

### Roles In Marketing
**Performance Marketers** — Manage paid advertising. Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, programmatic.

**Email/CRM Specialists** — Build email programs. Klaviyo, Dotdigital, automation, segmentation.

**Marketing Strategists** — Plan overall marketing approaches. Channel mix, budget allocation, campaign strategy.

**Paid Media Managers** — Execute and optimize paid campaigns. Daily management, optimization, reporting.

### Working With Marketing
- They need tracking and analytics — ensure measurement is in place
- They need landing pages and creative — collaborate on what they're driving traffic to
- They're data-driven — share performance insights
- They're budget-conscious — help them maximize ROI

## The SEO Team

SEO focuses on organic visibility in search engines.

### What SEO Does
- Conducts technical SEO audits and fixes
- Develops keyword and content strategies
- Optimizes on-page elements for search
- Builds authority through link earning
- Monitors and improves rankings

### Roles In SEO
**SEO Specialists** — Execute SEO tactics. Technical fixes, on-page optimization, monitoring.

**Content Strategists** — Plan content for search. Topic research, content calendars, editorial guidance.

**Technical SEO Analysts** — Deep dive on technical search issues. Site architecture, crawlability, Core Web Vitals.

### Working With SEO
- They need technical access and implementation support — their recommendations need developer time
- They need content collaboration — work with them on what gets published
- They think long-term — SEO is a marathon, not a sprint
- They need buy-in for best practices — advocate for SEO-friendly approaches

## The Data & Analytics Team

Data turns information into insights that drive decisions.

### What Data Does
- Implements tracking and analytics
- Builds reporting dashboards
- Analyzes performance and identifies insights
- Supports attribution and measurement
- Enables data-driven decision making

### Roles In Data
**Data Analysts** — Analyze data and produce insights. Reporting, analysis, recommendations.

**Analytics Engineers** — Build data infrastructure. Tracking implementation, data pipelines, dashboards.

**Reporting Specialists** — Create and maintain reports. Client reporting, automated dashboards, visualization.

### Working With Data
- They need clean data — ensure tracking is implemented correctly
- They need questions — tell them what you want to know
- They provide evidence — use their insights to inform decisions
- They're detail-oriented — appreciate their precision

## Operations and Support Teams

Beyond client-facing disciplines, operational teams keep Overdose running.

### People & Culture
Recruiting, onboarding, HR, employee experience. Your partners in career development and workplace issues.

### Finance
Invoicing, budgets, expenses. Handle your expense reports and understand commercial terms.

### Operations
Process improvement, resource allocation, project management infrastructure. Keep the engine running smoothly.

### IT
Internal systems, security, tools. Your help desk for tech issues.

## How Teams Come Together

The magic happens when disciplines collaborate:

**For a Platform Build:**
Strategy defines requirements → Experience designs the UX → Technology builds it → Marketing drives traffic → SEO optimizes for search → Data measures results

**For an Optimization Sprint:**
Data identifies opportunities → Strategy prioritizes → Experience designs tests → Technology implements → Marketing adjusts campaigns → Repeat

**For Ongoing Retainers:**
All disciplines work continuously, shifting focus based on priorities, with Strategy orchestrating the whole effort.

## Key Takeaways

- Strategy leads client relationships and ensures business alignment
- Experience designs how things work and look
- Technology builds and maintains the platforms
- Marketing drives traffic and customer relationships
- SEO builds organic visibility
- Data turns information into insights
- All disciplines collaborate to deliver Complete Commerce
